Q1
What is the difference between true state and observations?
True state is the real system value without errors while observations are measurements corrupted by noise.

Q2
How does estimation reduce noise?
Estimation (moving average) smooths random fluctuations by averaging multiple samples while random noise cancels out partially over time.

Q3
How does changing the noise level affect estimation?
Higher noise means that observations fluctuate more and estimation becomes less accurate and noisier. So, lower noise makes the estimate to track the true state better.

Q4
How does the window size affect the result?
Larger window means smoother estimate but more delay while a smaller window means less smoothing but faster response.
